- The biggest killer of young drivers is speeding -

The facts...

  • The biggest killer of young drivers is speeding 4
  • One-third of all road users in fatal crashes are young males, whilst 6% are young females 4
  • Young drivers compromise 25% of all Australian road deaths; however, are only about 10% of the licensed population 4
  • Road accidents are responsible for 45% of all young Australian injury deaths 4
  • Between 2014 and 2016 in NSW, drivers aged 20-24 were involved on average in 43 fatal crashes per year, higher than any other age group 3
  • Males compromise approximately 85% of all speed related fatalities and young male drivers make up half of this statistic 3
  • A 17 year old driver with a P1 licence is four times more likely to be involved in a fatal crash than a driver over 26 years 4

Young drivers are defined as 17 - 25 years old

Why...

are young drivers over represented in fatal crashes?

Young drivers are more at risk of fatal speeding accidents as they over-estimate their driving abilities, have a lack of experience, can be influenced by peers, generally drive unsafe cars and are less aware of the risks they are taking. 1


A survey by the Office of Road Safety found that 73% of young males admitted to deliberately speeding, revealing how this demographic is particularly vulnerable to over-estimating driving abilities. 1
